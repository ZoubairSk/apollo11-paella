The **Space Race** (roughly 1957–1975) was an accelerated innovation competition between the **USSR** and the **United States**, driven by the Cold War. It wasn’t only about “being first”, but about proving technological capacity, industrial organization, and scientific ambition.

## Why it still matters

- It sped up advances in **rocketry**, **computing**, **materials**, and **telecommunications**.
- It changed how society viewed science: space exploration became a **national project** and a cultural narrative.
- It laid foundations for later programs: space stations, weather satellites, GPS, and Earth observation.
