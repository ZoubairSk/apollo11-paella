The Soviet Union achieved several “firsts” that defined the early Space Race. Its program combined research, heavy industry, and highly symbolic goals with big public impact.

## Milestones that changed the game

- **Sputnik 1 (1957)**: first artificial satellite.
- **Laika (1957)**: first living being in orbit.
- **Yuri Gagarin (1961)**: first human in space.

## Strategy: impact and narrative

The USSR pursued achievements with strong public resonance. Each success strengthened the image of a system capable of producing cutting‑edge science and technology.

## Legacy

Beyond early milestones, the Soviet program contributed lasting engineering advances and long‑duration orbital flight experience, influencing later programs and international cooperation.
